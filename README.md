# ☀️ The Daily Morning Briefing

*A little gift from me to you.*

Every morning, instead of opening five apps and trying to remember what's on your plate, you paste one thing into Claude and get a clean, human summary of your day: what happened yesterday, what's on today, and the handful of things that actually need you.

I run this every morning and it genuinely changed how I start my day. Takes about 30 seconds to try. Here's everything you need.

---

## What you'll need

- **The Claude app** (desktop or mobile) — or [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) in a browser.
- **Optional but great:** connect your **email** and **calendar** to Claude. That's what lets it pull your real meetings and inbox. If you haven't connected them, no worries — the briefing still works, Claude will just ask you what's going on instead.

> Not sure if your email/calendar are connected? Just try Step 1 below. If Claude asks you what's on your calendar, that's your sign they aren't connected yet — and that's totally fine.

---

## Step 1 — Get your first briefing (do this now)

1. Open Claude.
2. Copy the entire box below.
3. Paste it into Claude and hit send.

```
You're my morning briefing assistant. Give me a clean, scannable briefing for today. Keep it tight and skip the fluff.

Pull from my connected email and calendar if you have access. If you don't, just ask me what's on my plate and build the briefing from my answer.

Format it exactly like this:

☀️ GOOD MORNING — [today's date]

📌 YESTERDAY
- 2 to 4 bullets on what actually mattered (key emails, meetings, anything that moved)

📅 TODAY
- My schedule in time order
- Flag anything I need to prep for before it starts

✅ NEEDS ME
- The 3 to 5 things genuinely worth my attention today, most important first
- Call out any buried or time-sensitive emails I might miss

🎯 ONE THING
- If I only get one thing done today, this is it

Rules: be direct, prioritize hard, and leave out anything that doesn't matter. If nothing is urgent, just say "nothing on fire — here's a clean day."
```

That's it. You now have a briefing on demand any time you want one.

---

## What you'll get back

Something like this:

> **☀️ GOOD MORNING — Monday, July 6**
>
> **📌 YESTERDAY**
> - Closed the loop with Sam on the venue — deposit is paid
> - Got a reply from the accountant; needs your signature this week
>
> **📅 TODAY**
> - 10:00 — Team standup (nothing to prep)
> - 1:30 — Dentist, 2nd & Main (leave by 1:10)
> - 4:00 — Call with Jordan — *he asked for the budget beforehand, have it ready*
>
> **✅ NEEDS ME**
> 1. Sign the accountant's form (blocking your filing)
> 2. Send Jordan the budget before 4:00
> 3. Reply to Mom — buried under newsletters from Friday
>
> **🎯 ONE THING**
> Sign that form. Everything else can slip a day; this can't.

Clean, honest, and actually useful. No 20-item to-do list — just the stuff that matters.

## Step 2 — Make it show up automatically (optional)

If you'd rather not paste it every morning, have Claude run it for you on a schedule. Just tell Claude:

```
Set up a scheduled task that runs my morning briefing prompt every day
at 7:00 AM and sends me the briefing.
```

Pick whatever time you actually wake up and check in. Claude handles the rest. (Scheduling is available in the Claude desktop app.)

## Make it yours

The prompt above is just a starting point. The more Claude knows about your life, the sharper the briefing gets. A few easy upgrades:

- **Name your VIPs:** *"Always flag anything from my partner, my boss, or my kid's school."*
- **Add a health nudge:** *"Add a 🏃 MOVE line with a reminder to work out or take a walk."*
- **Set the tone:** *"Keep it warm and encouraging"* — or *"Just the facts, no pep talk."*
- **Give it context:** Tell Claude what projects you're juggling and who the important people are. It'll start connecting dots on its own.
- **End on a good note:** *"Finish with one small thing I can look forward to today."*

Change anything. Break anything. It's yours now.

## Quick questions

**Do I need to pay for Claude?**
A free account works for the paste-and-run version. Scheduling it to run automatically needs the desktop app.

**Is my email/calendar data safe?**
Claude only reads what you connect, only when you ask. Nothing is posted or sent anywhere without you.

**It asked me what's on my calendar instead of just knowing. Why?**
That means your calendar isn't connected yet. Either connect it in Claude's settings, or just answer the question — the briefing works either way.

**Can I use this for the evening too?**
Yep. Copy the prompt, swap "morning" for "evening," and ask it to preview tomorrow instead of recap yesterday.
